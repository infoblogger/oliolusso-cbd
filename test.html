Cannabinoids Alleviate Symptoms of Bruxism

Bruxism or Tooth Grinding is a mouth-related disease in which your teeth clench or grind with each other. In Bruxism, your top and bottom teeth are tightly held with each other or you can simply say they slide back and forth over each other. It looks like a simple disease but it can cause severe impact if it is not treated in earlier stages. Bruxism can even cause Prostate Cancer when it remains for a long time.

Causes, symptoms, and risk factors of Bruxism

People can have bruxism without being aware and they clench and grind teeth at any time of day or night. Some people have Bruxism is sleep. It is the worst kind of Bruxism because you can't control it in sleep.

There is a very wide range of causes of Bruxism but medical officials are agreed on stress. Stress is the basic and most common reason for Bruxism. Stress can easily trigger the Bruxism in many people. Some people have Bruxism even without any causes. For example, people start clenching their teeth just sitting in loneliness. Bruxism causes pain also in some cases.

Doctors have agreed that Bruxism is a habit rather than normal reflex chewing activity. Regular chewing activity is always in response to some stimulus. For example, if you have food in your mouth, you will start chewing it but in the case of Bruxism, you will start clenching your teeth without any stimulus. Bruxism is a neuromuscular activity that can be controlled consciously, but Bruxism in sleep cannot be controlled very easily.

Apart from stress, Bruxism has the following possible causes,

  • Disturbed sleep
  • Malocclusion
  • High consumption of Caffeinated drinks such as coffee and colas
  • High consumption of Alcohol
  • Smoking
  • Anxiety and depression
  • Hypersensitivity of dopamine receptors in the brain
  • Parkinson’s disease
  • Obsessive-compulsive disorder

Now, we will discuss the symptoms of Bruxism. Bruxism has the following common symptoms.

  • Anxiety
  • Depression
  • Stress
  • Pain in jaws
  • Pain in Neck
  • Tinnitus
  • Gum inflammation
  • Insomnia
  • Headaches
